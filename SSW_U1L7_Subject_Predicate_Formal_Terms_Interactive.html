Formal Definitions
The academic language for sentence parts
🔍
SUBJECT
The part of the sentence that tells WHO or WHAT the sentence is about.
⚡
PREDICATE
The part of the sentence that tells what the subject DOES or IS. Always contains the verb.
Example: "The dog barked." — Subject = dog · Predicate = barked
Learn
Simple vs. Complete
Each sentence part has a simple and complete version
Subject Predicate
Simple Just the main noun Just the main verb
Complete Noun + all describing words Verb + all extra details
Example The big brown dog with floppy ears barked loudly at the mailman

Watch Out! Common Mix-Ups
These tricky spots trip up a lot of students.
⚠️ Simple vs. Complete
The simple subject is JUST the core noun — don't include "the," "a," or describing words!

✅ Simple subject of "The excited students" = students
❌ NOT "the excited students" — that's the complete subject.
⚠️ Where to Draw the Line
The dividing line goes right before the main verb. Everything from the verb onward is the complete predicate.

✅ Three brown dogs | ran quickly across the yard.
❌ NOT Three brown dogs ran | quickly across the yard.
