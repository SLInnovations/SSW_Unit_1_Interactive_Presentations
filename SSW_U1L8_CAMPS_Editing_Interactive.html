The CAMPS Editing Strategy

Check your writing FIVE times — once for each letter:

C

Capitals

Sentence beginnings, proper nouns, the word "I"

A

Agreement

Subjects and verbs match (singular ↔ singular, plural ↔ plural)

M

Missing Words

Read out loud — did you skip any words?

P

Punctuation

Every sentence ends with . ? or !

S

Spelling

Correct spelling, homophones (there/their/they're)

💡 CAMPS Tips & Tricks

🔑
One Check at a Time
Don't try to find everything at once! Read through five separate times, focusing on just ONE letter each time.
🎨
Color Code Your Edits
Use a different colored pencil for each check so you can see what types of errors you found.
🗣️
Read Out Loud for M Check
Your ears catch missing words that your eyes skip over. Reading aloud is the best trick for finding gaps!
🔄
Read Backwards for S Check
Reading backwards forces you to focus on each individual word instead of the meaning — great for catching spelling errors!

What does CAMPS stand for?
Capitals, Agreement, Missing Words, Punctuation, Spelling
How many times do you read through?
Five times — once for each letter of CAMPS. Focus on ONE type of error each time!
Why not check for everything at once?
Your brain gets overwhelmed and misses mistakes. Focusing on one thing at a time catches more errors!
Best trick for the M check?
Read out loud! Your ears catch missing words that your eyes skip over.
What does the A in CAMPS check for?
Agreement — making sure subjects and verbs match. Singular subjects → singular verbs. Plural → plural.
